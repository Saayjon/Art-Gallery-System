Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo di ser Piero da Vinci (15 April 1452 - 2 May 1519) was an Italian polymath of the High Renaissance who was active as a painter, draughtsman, engineer, scientist, theorist, sculptor, and architect. While his fame initially rested on his achievements as a painter, he has also become known for his notebooks, in which he made drawings and notes on a variety of subjects, including anatomy, astronomy, botany, cartography, painting, and palaeontology.

Mona Lisa

The Mona Lisa, a half-length portrait painted by Leonardo da Vinci between 1503 and 1506, is one of the most famous and iconic paintings in the world. It portrays a woman, believed to be Lisa Gherardini, the wife of a wealthy merchant, who commissioned the portrait. The Last Supper

The Last Supper (Italian: Il Cenacolo) is a late 15th-century mural painting by Leonardo da Vinci, created between 1495 and 1498. It was commissioned as part of a renovation project for the Convent of Santa Maria delle Grazie in Milan, Italy. The painting depicts the moment when Jesus Christ announces to His disciples that one of them will betray Him, an event known as the "betrayal of Christ."

Vitruvian Man

The Vitruvian Man is a world-renowned drawing by Leonardo da Vinci, created around 1490. It is based on the work of the ancient Roman architect Vitruvius, who believed that the proportions of the human body were a model of symmetry and beauty, reflecting the principles of harmony found in architecture and nature.

Annunciation

Annunciation is one of Leonardo da Vinci's earliest and most celebrated works, created around 1472-1475. It is an oil-on-wood panel painting that depicts the biblical moment when the Angel Gabriel announces to the Virgin Mary that she will conceive the Son of God, a pivotal event in Christian theology known as the Annunciation.
